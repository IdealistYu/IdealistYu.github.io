  1. Frequency Diversity: OFDM provides inherent frequency diversity by dividing the available frequency spectrum into multiple narrow subcarriers. Each subcarrier carries a lower data rate compared to the original wideband signal. This division of data across multiple subcarriers allows each subcarrier to experience different fading characteristics in a frequency selective fading channel. As a result, the impact of fading on individual subcarriers is reduced, and the overall system performance is improved.

  2. Equalization: OFDM employs a guard interval between consecutive OFDM symbols. This guard interval contains redundant samples and acts as a guard against intersymbol interference (ISI) caused by multipath propagation. The guard interval enables efficient equalization techniques to mitigate the effects of frequency selective fading. By removing the ISI, the receiver can recover the transmitted symbols accurately, even in the presence of fading.

  3. Orthogonality: In OFDM, the subcarriers are carefully designed to be orthogonal to each other, meaning that they do not interfere with one another. The orthogonality property allows the subcarriers to be closely spaced without causing significant interference, resulting in efficient spectrum utilization. In a frequency selective fading channel, different subcarriers experience varying levels of fading due to their different carrier frequencies. However, since the subcarriers are orthogonal, the fading on one subcarrier does not affect the others significantly, thereby reducing the impact of fading on the overall signal.

  4. Efficient Channel Equalization: OFDM simplifies channel equalization by converting the frequency-selective fading channel into a set of parallel flat fading channels, each corresponding to a subcarrier. Since flat fading channels are easier to equalize, OFDM simplifies the equalization process compared to other modulation techniques. Equalization techniques, such as frequency-domain equalization or time-domain equalization, can be efficiently applied to each subcarrier to combat fading.

Overall, the combination of frequency diversity, guard interval, orthogonality, and efficient equalization techniques makes OFDM a suitable choice for coping with frequency selective fading channels. It enables reliable data transmission in wireless communication systems, such as mobile networks, where fading is a common occurrence due to multipath propagation and other environmental factors.

  1. 周波数多様性の利用: OFDMは、周波数領域を多くの独立したサブキャリア（個別の周波数帯域）に分割します。これにより、異なる周波数帯域でフェージングが発生しても、他の周波数帯域では影響を受けずに信号が伝送されます。つまり、一部のサブキャリアがフェージングによって影響を受けても、他のサブキャリアが引き続き正常に伝送されるため、伝送の信頼性が向上します。

  2. インターシンボルインターフェアレンスの軽減: インターシンボルインターフェアレンス（ISI）は、伝搬路の遅延やマルチパス伝播によって発生する問題です。OFDMでは、シンボル時間を短くし、それぞれのサブキャリアが独立しているため、ISIの影響を軽減することができます。各サブキャリアは短い時間幅で送信されるため、遅延の影響を最小限に抑えることができます。

  3. 高いスペクトル効率: OFDMは、周波数帯域を効果的に利用することができます。サブキャリアは互いに直交しているため、サブキャリア間の干渉を最小限に抑えながら、高いデータレートを実現することができます。これにより、限られた周波数帯域で高速なデータ伝送を行うことが可能となります。
